Target Audience

Persona, behaviors & frustrations.

35–55 knowledge workers who want to take a proactive approach to their health. Data-oriented, highly engaged in learning, and motivated by prevention — they want modern care that combines education, biomarker visibility, and better context than traditional healthcare usually provides.

Mindset
Preventive, curious & self-directed
Rather than waiting for symptoms to escalate, they actively seek education, follow current health trends, and want to understand the why behind each intervention.
What Frustrates Them
Rushed, reactive, and under-explained care
Traditional healthcare feels rushed and reactive. New health technology moves faster than the guidance patients receive. They're left to connect lab results and self-tracking on their own, without a trusted medical translator.
Why They Convert

Primary needs & willingness to pay.

They do not want to replace doctors. They want a more informed relationship with healthcare, where modern data leads to clear action and physician access is still available when it matters.

The Three Pillars

How each pillar works.

01
Pillar One
Two tracks, one clinical engine

Longevity Telehealth operates on two distinct tracks designed for different member relationships — both anchored in the same physician-led, prevention-first model.

Transactional Track
MSP-Covered Primary Care
No commitment, no program. Accessible virtual visits for members who want quality longevity-aware primary care without signing up for a full program. Builds trust and lowers the barrier to entry.
Optimization Program
Annual Membership
A structured annual program with recurring physician relationships built around metabolic health, hormones, cardiovascular risk, and long-term healthspan strategy. The relationship that drives retention and outcomes.

Core care model (Optimization Program)

1
Assessment
Intake, history, symptoms, goals, labs, and baseline wearable patterns
2
Plan
Personalized care plan with screening, treatment, coaching priorities, and biomarkers to watch
3
Ongoing Management
Dose adjustments, repeat testing, progress reviews, and long-term optimization
02
Pillar Two
In-house pharmacy, deliberately narrow

Not a full retail pharmacy. Instead, a curated online medication source tied directly to the clinical program, focused on therapies most relevant to the member base. GLP-1 medications, hormone therapies, and selected companion treatments.

Design principles

Curated only: Medications that fit the preventive and longevity thesis
Safety first: Built around eligibility screening, monitoring, and refill discipline
Integrated: Kept within physician oversight and lab review workflows
Part of care delivery, not a commodity storefront — simpler fulfillment, fewer handoffs
03
Pillar Three
A longevity data portal

Most people have fragmented health data and no operating system for it. The portal becomes the member's home base: one place to understand what is happening, what has changed, and what to do next.

Wearables: Import sleep, activity, heart rate, recovery, and other daily signals into one view
Tests & Labs: Track biomarker history, trend lines, testing cadence, and what each result means
Education: Translate clinical recommendations into understandable next steps, protocols, and habits
Lab trends Wearable signals Biomarker history Education modules Behaviour change Symptom tracking Medication adherence Longitudinal dashboard

HsCRP
High-Sensitivity C-Reactive Protein
Your value: 1.8 mg/L — borderline
Inflammation
What is it
HsCRP is a protein produced by the liver in response to inflammation. The high-sensitivity version of the test detects low-level chronic inflammation — the kind that accelerates cardiovascular disease and metabolic dysfunction even when you feel fine.
Why it matters for longevity
Elevated HsCRP is independently associated with increased risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and all-cause mortality. Tracking it over time reveals whether your lifestyle and treatment protocols are reducing systemic inflammation.
What brings it down
Sleep quality, omega-3 intake, exercise regularity, reducing ultra-processed food, and managing excess body weight all correlate with reduced CRP. Your physician can review which factors are most relevant to your profile.

MH
What's the difference between CRP and HsCRP?
VP
Great question. Standard CRP is used in hospitals to detect acute infections and major inflammation — it measures higher ranges (above 10 mg/L). HsCRP uses a more sensitive assay that detects chronic low-grade inflammation in the 0.5–10 mg/L range. That's the range relevant to cardiovascular and metabolic risk, which is why it's the version used in longevity programs like yours. AI
MH
How does my sleep affect my CRP?
VP
Sleep has a direct effect on inflammation. Consistently sleeping under 6 hours has been shown in multiple studies to increase HsCRP, while sleep fragmentation — even at normal duration — elevates inflammatory cytokines. Your Oura data shows your sleep efficiency has improved over the past 6 weeks, which likely contributes to your CRP trending down. Your physician can confirm whether this correlation is showing in your numbers at your next review. AI

WHAT IT MEASURES
Epigenetic clocks like DunedinPACE and Horvath's clock analyze DNA methylation patterns across thousands of genomic sites to estimate how fast your cells are aging — independent of your calendar age.
HOW IT'S USED IN YOUR PROGRAM
VitalPath includes epigenetic age testing at program baseline and at 12-month milestones. Your physician interprets the result in the context of your full biomarker picture — not as a standalone score.
WHAT MOVES THE NUMBER
Sleep, exercise, inflammatory load, metabolic health, and stress are all independently shown to affect epigenetic aging rate. The program interventions your physician builds are designed to move these drivers directly.
A NOTE ON INTERPRETATION
Epigenetic age is a directional signal, not a verdict. Your physician contextualizes it alongside your labs, wearables, and clinical picture — and explains what it means for your specific care plan at each program review.
